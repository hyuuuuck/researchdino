# UI Concept: Living Laboratory Map

## Main Idea

The UI should make the research workflow visible as a laboratory. Each room
represents a stage in the research process, and work items move between rooms.

## Rooms

- Collection Dock: PDFs, DOI inputs, and web search results enter the system.
- Reading Bench: papers are parsed, summarized, and converted into claim cards.
- Debate Room: agents discuss evidence, contradictions, methods, and weaknesses.
- Leader Office: the user or leader agent approves, rejects, or redirects outputs.
- Library: approved knowledge is stored, indexed, and made searchable.
- Strategy Room: research gaps and paper ideas are generated.
- Experiment Bay: experiments, controls, readouts, and risks are designed.
- Writing Studio: manuscript outlines and sections are drafted with citations.

## Work Items

- Paper Card: a paper currently being processed.
- Claim Card: a specific scientific claim with source evidence.
- Gap Card: an unresolved contradiction or missing experiment.
- Hypothesis Card: a possible research direction.
- Experiment Card: a proposed experiment plan.
- Manuscript Card: a section, paragraph, or outline node.

## Interaction Model

The center of the screen shows the laboratory map. A user can click a room or
work item to open a detail panel.

The detail panel should show:

- current status
- assigned agents
- source papers
- extracted claims
- debate transcript
- leader decision
- stored knowledge links
- next recommended action

## Visual Priority

This should feel like a serious research cockpit with a playful lab metaphor.
The map is not decoration; it is the primary process visualization layer.

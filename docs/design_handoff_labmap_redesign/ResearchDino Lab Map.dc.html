Abstract

Accumulation of misfolded α-synuclein is a pathological hallmark of Parkinson's disease. Macroautophagy is a principal route for clearing these aggregates, yet the upstream signals that gate autophagosome formation in dopaminergic neurons remain incompletely defined.

Here we show that phosphorylation of ULK1 at Ser757 is both necessary and rate-limiting for aggregate clearance in primary midbrain cultures. CRISPR knockout of ULK1 reduced clearance by 61% under identical stimulation, and the effect was selective for the dopaminergic subtype.

Selective vulnerability of substantia nigra neurons has long been attributed to their high metabolic demand and reliance on efficient protein turnover. Autophagy as a Therapeutic Axis in α-Synucleinopathies: A Mechanistic Review

Smith J.¹, Alvarez R.¹, Tanaka K.²  ·  Draft compiled by Writer agent
Abstract

α-Synucleinopathies share a common failure of protein clearance. This review synthesizes recent mechanistic evidence that positions ULK1-gated macroautophagy as a tractable therapeutic axis, with particular attention to the selective vulnerability of dopaminergic neurons and the kinase circuitry that initiates autophagosome formation under proteostatic stress.

1  Introduction

The progressive loss of substantia nigra neurons defines the motor phenotype of Parkinson's disease. Among the proposed drivers, impaired autophagic clearance of misfolded α-synuclein has emerged as both a mechanistic cause and a therapeutic opportunity.
